## 1. What is Daily GPS?

Daily GPS is a multi-AI productivity system I designed over eight months of daily use,
testing, improving, iterating, and evolving with my AI crew: First Mate Yolanda
(ChatGPT) and Quartermaster Claude (Claude). I initially started building it to see how
much more I could get done each day by using AI tools to work faster than ever before.
Over time, I realized that while I was getting a lot more work done, I was also getting
way out of balance. I love surfing, swimming, yoga, lifting weights, meditating, spending
time at the beach with my dog, and DJ’ing as a creative outlet, but all of those things
were getting drowned out week after week.
So I set a goal with my AI crew to evolve the rough idea into a real system. My goal was
simple. I wanted to build a business without giving up my life in the process.
What we ended up with is a way for me to design my days in line with all of my goals,
both personal and professional. The result is that I can live and work authentically and
consistently, day after day and week after week.
What has surprised me most is how different my life feels now. After trying countless
productivity systems over the last four decades, this is the first time it has felt easy and
sustainable. I finally feel like I am living and working on purpose, by design.
The nautical language in this guide, including the name Daily GPS, is simply how I
chose to make the system feel like mine. You can do the same by using your own
language, themes, and style so the system fits who you are.
This System Uses Two AI Assistants
ChatGPT (I call mine "First Mate Yolanda"): She handles drafts, meaning-making,
automated tasks, and notifications.
Claude (I call mine "Quartermaster Claude"): He handles document creation, precision
formatting, and tracking.
At the top of this crew, I’m my own "Captain" and make all the final decisions. And I let
the crew handle all of the cognitive load so I can focus on the things only I can do.
This guide is designed to help you do the same for yourself and also to customize it in a
way that perfectly fits with who you are and how you work best while also having fun.
Why Two AIs?
First, two AI perspectives create a unique, exponential output from their two very
different skill sets and strengths. Additionally, by using two AIs I’ve learned over many
months that they each generate new ideas as they iterate as a team with each other
and also with me to create outputs that are, well, kind of beyond what any one of us
could create by ourselves. We like to say, "1+1+1 = 10."

---

Next, between the three of us working together as a team, I’ve experienced that we
catch blind spots that I or a single AI would miss.
In terms of specific strengths of how Daily GPS runs and works, the high-level basics
are:
ChatGPT can run scheduled tasks and send notifications. Claude can’t do that yet.
ChatGPT also has more of a "human touch" and creative eloquence.
Claude creates beautifully formatted .docx documents and tracks every detail to
perfection. ChatGPT doesn’t do this well. But, at this time, Claude isn’t able to auto-run
tasks and send notifications to me like ChatGPT can.
The result? You’ll see in this guide that the "First Watch" and "Night Watch" logs that
require automations, notifications, and automated initial eloquent drafts, they are all
initiated in ChatGPT. They are then handed off to Claude who reviews, refines and
inserts the final, perfectly detailed document output that is the essence of Daily GPS.
Think of it as an AI-powered journaling system that gives you the intelligence and
support of AI to reach your goals and stay on track consistently.
With that said, here’s a quick overview.
What You’ll Build
- ​
First Watch — a morning intention planning ritual
- ​
Hourly GPS — quick check-ins during your work day
- ​
Movement Tracker — physical movement or daily habit tracking
- ​
DIM / Squirrel Wrangler — idea capture without derailment
- ​
Priority Engine — automatic analysis of captured ideas
- ​
Night Watch — an evening reflection that closes the day
- ​
Sunday Evening Post — a weekly recap that synthesizes your results
And finally, the optional enhancement below is how I personally get the most out of
Daily GPS:
- ​

## 2. Installing Daily GPS

Take your time with this section. The setup is very detailed and takes around
30-60 minutes. Just follow it step by step methodically. Once installed, Daily
GPS runs smoothly and only requires 20-30 minutes per day: 10-15 in the
morning and evening, plus some quick check-ins during the day.
You don’t need to understand the entire system before you begin. Daily GPS is
designed to be learned by doing. Also, I want to note early that it takes around two
weeks of steady use for the system (and you) to evolve and start seeing and feeling
results. What I’ve also noticed is that every two to three weeks seems to have even
further integration and evolution as both the AIs and you learn and grow working
together.
If I could give one piece of "must do" advice at this stage, commit to using the system
consistently each day for at least two weeks.
In the beginning, I fully admit that I was using it here and there. My results were
interesting, but nothing earth shattering. At one point, I switched to a daily routine. That
one simple shift changed my results massively. And at four weeks? I had achieved more
than in the last four months. So I learned the hard way and am sharing it with you here
that the key is to get two solid weeks in to grow and evolve the system as a team and
with you.
Next, this section walks you through the one-time setup so you can run your first day.
The full installation takes about an hour, including customizing your files. Everything
else builds from there. I spend around twenty minutes in the morning and the evening
handling the First Watch and Night Watch process, and only a few minutes here and
there throughout the day for the Hourly GPS and the DIMs process.

## 5. Hourly GPS (Focus Check-Ins)

Hourly GPS is the spine of Daily GPS. Without it, the system doesn’t work.
Here’s why: First Watch sets your intentions. Night Watch reflects on what happened.
But Hourly GPS is what actually captures what you did: the actions, the drift, the wins,
the course corrections. Without this middle piece, Night Watch becomes guesswork
instead of real reflection.
Where: Claude only, in your Hourly GPS 1.0 thread
The Logic
- ​
First Watch = Intentions (what you plan to do)
- ​
Hourly GPS = Actions (what you actually do)
- ​
Night Watch = Reflection (reviewing what Hourly GPS captured)
This is the chain that makes Daily GPS work. Skipping Hourly GPS can break the chain
somewhat. Some days are busier than others, when this happens, I at least try to do
one end of day summary of hours worked and on what throughout the day. While this
works, it’s not nearly as easy, detailed or effective as quick check-ins throughout the
day.

### How It Works

Check in between work blocks, or roughly every hour. The goal is awareness, not a
timer. Even a 1-minute voice note counts.
1.​ Open Claude’s Hourly GPS thread
2.​ Report in: what time it is, what you’re doing, any drift, any wins and what you
plan on doing in the next work block
3.​ Claude tracks it and reflects back patterns
That’s it. No complicated tracking. Just quick honest check-ins throughout your day.
Pro Tip: Try using a pomodoro timer (25 minutes on, 5 minutes off) and check in with
Claude during each break.
What You Might Say
"Starting deep work on the sales page. Phone is away."
"Got distracted by email for 20 minutes. Back on track now."
"Finished the first draft. Taking a 10-minute break."
"Feeling scattered. Not sure what to focus on next."
What Claude Does With This

---

Claude tracks your check-ins and spots patterns you might miss: when you drift, what
pulls you off track, what times of day you’re most focused. This all feeds into Night
Watch, giving you real data instead of fuzzy memories.
Claude doesn’t judge. Claude tracks. At the end of the day, you’ll have an honest
picture of how you actually spent your time. This system is what inspired the saying we
use: "Observe the seas, don’t judge the sailor."

## 7. DIMs and the Squirrel Wrangler

DIM stands for "Distractions, Ideas, Miscellaneous." We like to call ours the "Daily Idea
Manifest" in line with our nautical theme. It’s how you capture anything that could derail
your focus without actually chasing it.
The phrase "DIM it" itself takes the spotlight off the idea: you capture it, diminish its pull,
and return to work.
We like to think of it this way:
"Observe the squirrel. Don’t chase the squirrel."
Where DIMs Live
Tracked DIMs live in Claude only in your DIM Ledger 1.0 thread. This is the only place
where DIMs are processed due to Claude’s skill at tracking every detail that honestly,
ChatGPT just doesn’t do well. While ChatGPT might ask if you want to DIM something,
you would then take the idea and log it in the Claude DIM Ledger 1.0 chat thread.
Where DIMs Come From
DIMs come from two sources:
- ​
Your own ideas — something pops into your head during focused work
- ​
AI suggestions — ChatGPT or Claude offer options or next steps, and you want
to save one for later
In both cases, your AI crew is trained to help you spot potential squirrels. They might
say "That sounds like a squirrel, want to DIM it?" or "Great idea, let’s DIM this and stay
focused."
How to Capture a DIM
1.​ Open Claude’s DIM Ledger thread
2.​ Paste or speak the idea: "DIM this: [your idea, category, and urgency if known]"
3.​ Return to work immediately
That’s it. Claude handles everything else.
What Claude Does Automatically
When you capture a DIM, Claude immediately:
- ​
Records and categorizes it
- ​
Runs it through the Priority Engine
- ​
Makes a recommendation: Do, Defer, Delegate, or Delete
The Urgent Exception

---

If something is clearly urgent, blocking your work, or trivial and fast, just do it. Tell
Claude you’re handling it now. No guilt, no penalty. Move on.
Night Watch: Review and Approve
During Night Watch, Claude shows you two sections:
- ​
Completed DIMs: what you finished today
- ​
Open DIMs: each with Claude’s recommendation already made
You review Claude’s recommendations and approve or edit. Claude does the thinking.
You make the final call.
First Watch: Automatic Carryover
- ​
Completed DIMs from the day before drop off automatically
- ​
Open DIMs carry forward into your next First Watch
Sunday Evening Post: Weekly View
Each week, your Sunday Evening Post includes:
- ​
Completed DIMs from the week: celebrate these

## 8. Priority Engine

The Priority Engine is a decision framework that automatically analyzes your DIMs so
you don’t have to.
When It Runs
Automatically, every time you capture a DIM. Claude applies your priorities without you
having to think.
The Filters
The Priority Engine evaluates each DIM against filters that you customized during
installation. For reference, the Priority Filters we use are below:
- ​
Revenue impact — does this drive revenue?
- ​
Urgency — when is this needed?
- ​
Alignment — does this support your current mission?
- ​
Effort — how heavy is this task?
Then, based on this analysis, Claude recommends: Do, Defer, Delegate, or Delete.
From there you can decide the final action to take with DIMs, let Claude know in the
DIM Ledger 1.0 chat thread, and he’ll make the updates.

How It All Connects
Here’s the simple flow of Daily GPS so you always know what’s happening behind the
scenes:
1.​ You capture ideas, tasks, and obligations and log them in your DIM thread with
Claude as they arise.​
2.​ Claude categorizes and tracks each DIM in a structured mini-ledger.​
3.​ During Night Watch, open DIMs are reviewed and run through the Priority
Engine, and the Night Watch summary is finalized with those recommendations
included.​
4.​ Completed DIMs listed in your Night Watch drop out of the “wake effect” system
automatically and don’t get carried into the next morning’s First Watch.​
5.​ Any open DIMs on Night Watch then flow forward into the next morning’s First
Watch so nothing gets lost.​
6.​ On your Sunday Evening Post weekly recap, you review and celebrate wins from
the week. Note that open DIMs are not reviewed there. They continue flowing
naturally through the daily Night Watch → First Watch cycle.​

## 12. Continuation Prompts

Chat threads eventually hit their capacity limit. When that happens, you’ll need to create
a new thread and use a continuation prompt to carry context forward.
When You’ll Need These
- ​
Claude with .docx output: about 1 week of usage hits the chat thread limit
- ​
Claude without docs: 2+ weeks
- ​
ChatGPT: 1-3 months or more
How to Check a Chat Thread Limit
This is pretty important in Claude. Check before each session after you’ve been running
First Watch and Night Watch for about four or five days. Type or say something like
"Hey Claude, how are we doing on the chat thread limit here?" If Claude responds that
you’re close to the limit it’s time to start a new chat thread.
How to Continue
When a thread is getting full, simply ask the AI:
"Please write me a continuation prompt I can use to start a new
thread [new thread name] and pick up where we left off in this
[current thread name] because we are almost at the limit."
For example, if I’m in the First Watch 1.0 chat thread, the "new thread name" I would
use is "First Watch 2.0" and the "current thread name" is of course "First Watch 1.0."
The AI will generate a custom prompt based on your actual context. This is better than a
rigid template because:
- ​
It’s tailored to exactly what you’ve been working on
- ​
No copy/paste formatting errors
- ​
It adapts to whatever workflow you’ve built
- ​
You learn the skill rather than relying on a script
Then create a new thread (e.g., "First Watch 2.0"), paste the continuation prompt, and
keep going.
Pre-built continuation prompts for First Watch, Night Watch, and SEP are also available
in your Resource Folder.
Open Your Daily GPS Resource Folder
If You Hit a Chat Thread Limit

---

In the event you accidentally hit a chat thread limit, you won’t be able to type or talk
within that chat thread. Don’t worry, this happens to me all the time despite my best
efforts.
There’s an easy workaround as the AIs are still able to access and view the chat thread
that hit the limit. When this happens, just open a new chat thread and type:
"Hey Claude, we hit the chat limit in [old thread name]. Please
write me a continuation prompt I can use in this new chat
thread [new thread name] so we can pick up where we left off."
The AI will then output a prompt for you to copy and paste into the chat window to kick
things off again.
Recreating Automated Tasks
When you create a new continuation thread in ChatGPT, you must also update your
automated tasks. As a reminder, these are three tasks for automating and sending you
notifications for First Watch, Night Watch, and the Sunday Evening Post. The old task is
still pointing to the old thread, so start by deleting the old task:
1.​ Open desktop ChatGPT in a web browser
2.​ Go to Tasks / Scheduled automations
3.​ Find and delete all tasks pointing to the old thread
4.​ Open your new continuation thread (e.g., First Watch 2.0)
5.​ Create new tasks by following the steps and using the task creation prompts from
the beginning of this Guide: 2. Installing Daily GPS, Part D: Set Up Automated
Tasks (ChatGPT).
Important: Deleting old tasks can only be done from desktop ChatGPT in a web
browser, not from ChatGPT mobile apps.

## 13. Troubleshooting

Missing a First Watch or Night Watch Log
Since the automated tasks for First Watch and Night Watch use "wake effect" for
continuity, sometimes if you miss finalizing a Night Watch log and loading it into
ChatGPT, the next morning’s First Watch Log won’t have the correct information to pull
in from the night before.
There’s nothing to worry about, this happens to me a few times a month if I have an
event or holiday and I don’t get around to finalizing Night Watch. There are two easy
fixes and I prefer the first one.
1.​ I simply go back and finish the Night Watch log draft and finalization then upload
it to ChatGPT. Then, I tell ChatGPT something like, "Hey Yolanda, can you
please re-run today’s First Watch [date] again using the Night Watch [date] log
that I just uploaded to your files? Thank you." The reason I like this method is
because it fills in any holes in the logs for the week so that the Sunday Evening
Post has all the documents it needs to look back at the full week, and also, it
keeps a nice neat ledger of a log for every day in my Google Drive folder where I
store all of my First Watch, Night Watch, and Sunday Evening Post documents.
2.​ You can just "skip" the Night Watch document from the night before. Pick up with
the current First Watch draft and just fill in the blanks as you see fit. It leaves a
small hole in the week relative to the Sunday Evening Post, etc. but it’s not a big
deal in the grand scheme of things over the long haul of many months or a year.
Chat Thread Limits
This is pretty important in Claude. Check before each session after you’ve been running
First Watch, Night Watch or other chat threads for about four or five days. Say
something like "Hey Claude, how are we doing on the chat thread limit here?" When
you get close to the limits, use the continuation prompt approach from Section 12.
The js_md_docx Skill
Make sure this document is uploaded to your Claude project. Without it, Claude hits
chat thread limits in 1-2 days but by using it, your chat threads extend to about a week
in Claude.
Drafts Not Generating
Check that tasks are set up correctly in desktop ChatGPT. Make sure the task says
"generate" not "remind." Also verify the task was created inside the correct chat thread.
Quality Feels Off

---

Start a fresh thread and re-initialize using the proper prompts as needed from 2.
Installing Daily GPS near the beginning of this Guide. Sometimes a clean start is the
fastest fix.
Automated Task Not Working
If your First Watch or Night Watch draft isn’t appearing:
- ​
Confirm the task exists: Open desktop ChatGPT → Tasks / Scheduled
automations
- ​
Confirm it’s in the right thread (not a random conversation)
- ​
Confirm the scheduled time hasn’t passed already for today
- ​
Try deleting and recreating the task inside the correct thread
